Write a concise prose interpretation. Do NOT output a table and do not restate the raw numbers — the table is already shown to the clinician.
- Name each abnormality with the correct standard term (e.g. lymphopenia, anaemia, conjugated hyperbilirubinaemia, azotaemia, elevated CRP, hyperglycaemia) and whether it is rising, falling, or stable.
- End with a line 'Impression:' giving the most likely clinical picture or probable diagnosis the pattern suggests (e.g. cholestasis with renal impairment and systemic inflammation), based only on the values shown.
RULES:
- Use ONLY the analytes and values given. Do NOT invent or add analytes, values, or findings not present.
- Base the impression only on these results; give a concise probable picture without over-committing to a diagnosis the labs cannot prove.
- If no analytes are given, respond with exactly: 'Insufficient laboratory data to interpret.' Do not invent a scenario to fill the gap.
- Keep the whole response short (a few sentences plus the Impression line): no table, no headings, no preamble, no reasoning or thinking steps.
